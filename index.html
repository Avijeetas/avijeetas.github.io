Research

I am a Graduate Research Assistant working on semantic query interpretation for Question Answering systems. My work emphasizes interpretability and explainability, and has been accepted at RANLP (Recent Advances in Natural Language Processing).

Previously, I served as a Graduate Teaching Assistant for Capstone Projects, Software Engineering, where I managed over 50 projects and guided students through the SDLC, Agile, and Scrum pipelines, developing critical project management and leadership skills, used my full-stack development knowledge.

As a Ph.D. student at the University of North Texas, I work under the supervision of Dr. Wei Jin in the domains of Text and Web Data Mining, Information Retrieval, and Biomedical and Healthcare Informatics. I am also involved in the Text and Web Mining Lab as a research assistant.

My research focuses on the following areas:

  • Query Reformulation in Search Engines
  • Reinforcement Learning and its application to search query optimization
  • Large Language Models (LLMs) for knowledge extraction and information retrieval
  • Knowledge Graphs for enhancing search relevance and semantic search
  • Applied Machine Learning to improve data-driven decision-making processes

You can view my research publications on my Google Scholar profile.

Before my doctoral studies, I earned my B.Sc. in Computer Science and Engineering from Chittagong University of Engineering and Technology (CUET) in June 2021. I gained valuable industry experience at W3Eden (WordPress plugin management), REVE Systems (enterprise software solutions for the Bangladesh Parliament), and BKash (developing AutoPay features for fintech). At BKash, I worked on scalable systems that handled recurring payments, bill payments, and mobile recharge for millions of users.
